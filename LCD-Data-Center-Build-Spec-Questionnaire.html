MTP Trunk / Backbone Cables

High-fiber-count point-to-point backbone, typically MTP/MPO-terminated for cassette or direct connection.

Ask: "Plenum air-handling space, or not?" Plenum (OFNP) is the safe default for custom DC builds.

Fiber Array / Harness Cables

MTP/MPO-to-LC fan-out/breakout harnesses for parallel optics.

Ask: what the MTP breaks out to — duplex LC for switch ports is most common; simplex for certain patching.

Ask: "Plenum space, or not?" Plenum (OFNP) is the safe default for custom builds.

◆ Confirm before we build

Note: the MTP end is usually Female; the LC end has no gender. Confirm against their equipment.

Fiber Patch Cords (Jumpers)

Equipment and cross-connect jumpers — LC primarily, plus SC and small-form connectors.

Ask: what plugs into the equipment. LC is the default; SC and small-form (MDC/SN/CS) show up on high-density gear.

APC (green, angled) for singlemode/low-reflectance. UPC for most multimode.

◆ Confirm before we build

IEC Data Center Power Cords

Rack/PDU/device power. Plug end, device end, gauge and rating drive the part.

Ask: what it plugs into — a PDU (C14/C20 plug) or a wall/UPS (NEMA). That decides End A.

C15/C21 are notched high-temp variants required by some switches/PDUs.

Copper Patch Cords

Cat6 / Cat6A RJ45 jumpers — category, shielding and jacket rating drive the part.

Ask: Cat6A is the DC default (10GBASE-T, dense/hot runs); Cat6 only for 1G or short hops.

Optical Transceivers

Pluggable optics. OEM compatibility coding is the make-or-break spec — capture the exact platform.

Coding must match the host switch/NIC or the optic won't be recognized.

Ask: "What's the exact OEM SKU you're replacing?" That's what we cross-reference — get it, don't guess.

Make-or-break: get the switch/NIC and OS/firmware. If they ask "will it work on my Ciscos / void support?" — it's coded to their platform, MSA-compliant, validated against firmware, and third-party optics don't void the hardware warranty.

◆ Confirm before we build

7

AOCs & DACs

Active Optical Cables and Direct Attach Copper. Length drives passive-vs-active; both ends can be coded differently.

Passive DAC ≤3–5m. Beyond that, active DAC or AOC.

Ends can connect different vendors — capture both if so.

Ask: the exact SKU(s) being matched — host SKU, plus the branch SKU on breakouts.

Make-or-break: both ends — what switch/NIC at A and B, and firmware. That's what compatibility is validated against.
